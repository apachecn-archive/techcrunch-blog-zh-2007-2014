# 十条你认为很酷、很有见地但实际上不然的评论

评论是 TechCrunch 的生命线，我们热爱它们。但我们也看到了一些怪人、阴谋论者和混蛋，他们有话要说，而且相信宪法或上帝赋予他们的权利说出来，就在这里。作者认为这些评论既有趣又有见地，但当我们一遍又一遍地看到同样的评论时，我们就笑不出来了。

下面是我们最不喜欢的十种评论类型。你是哪一个？

1.  *“Slow news day?”* – Typically left on stories that the reader thinks are boring, not newsworthy or off topic. A recent example is the [Britney on Twitter](https://web.archive.org/web/20230213024820/https://techcrunch.com/2008/10/19/omg-britney/) story – early on someone made this insightful comment and the story ended up with 112 comments. This comment is left at least once per day on some story, and usually multiple times per day. We usually delete them.

    在 TechCrunch，我们从来没有过一个缓慢的新闻日。我总是有一个十个或更多的职位写的名单，我只是寻找的时间，以达到他们。如果一个故事在网站上，这意味着我们希望它在那里。

2.  *“最近 TechCrunch 确实在走下坡路。”*-2005 年第一次离开，博客推出几个月后。从那以后每天都能看到。
3.  *[random trolling, often with a wish that we’d die or are unethical in some way]* – We get lots of these, and delete as many of them as we can. But first we check the IP address against previous comments left on the site. About once a month we see a really nasty anonymous comment that’s left by an IP address that had always been used by a single named commenter before that. Most of the time we had just posted a critical review of the person’s company right before the comment was left.

    我们不会公布这些人的真实姓名，但我有一份名单，里面都是在某些方面确实感到不安的人。在某个活动中看到他们表现得好像真的认为 TechCrunch 很棒，这通常很有意思。

    如果你要说一些令人讨厌的话，请使用你的真实姓名，或者了解代理服务器的魔力

4.  “*SoAndSo already did this”* – A comment left when the reader believes that the new service we are describing is not a new idea, and therefore shouldn’t be given any attention. The problem is that there is very rarely a brand new idea. Instead, most of the products we review are iterations on what’s come before. And sometimes a new product tries to tackle the same problem that someone else has in a new way.

    虽然其他与新产品相似或相关的产品也值得一提，但简单地说它们是其他产品的模仿品就没什么意思了。如果我们在报道它，我们认为它在某种程度上是有趣或独特的。

5.  *“Nice journalism…where’s the balance?”* This comment, which comes in many forms, criticizes us for writing a one-sided story. People are used to reading old media, where journalists don’t write their opinions. Instead, they get sources to say what they want. I’ve seen this first hand – being interviewed for half an hour or more on a topic and then seeing a single, misleading quote in the finished product.

    我们不努力保持平衡。我们力求做到正确。我们也不会试图欺骗读者，让他们认为某个消息来源说了他们实际上没有说的话。

6.  *“How much did the company pay you to write this post?”* This is a conspiracy theory comment, bred in the minds of people who only see evil in the world. Think through this for a moment…if we ever asked for or received payment for a post, how long would it take for someone to talk? We are stubbornly independent, and our opinions are our own. If someone offered to pay us for a post, we’d just [publish the offer immediately](https://web.archive.org/web/20230213024820/http://www.crunchnotes.com/2007/04/10/hmmmno/) and humiliate the company.

    说到广告商，我们在销售和编辑之间有一堵严格的道德墙，就像“真实”媒体一样。你可以在 TechCrunch 上买到你喜欢的所有广告，但它不会给你带来社论式的报道。

7.  *“What’s your problem with [Company X]”* – Often left when we critically cover Yahoo, Microsoft, AOL or Google. Most recently it’s been Yahoo, which has been mismanaged beyond negligence this year. These comments are particularly funny when its alter ego (see immediately below) is written after a positive story on the same company.

    我写下我的想法，然后写下我为什么这么想。如果你不同意，很好。但这并不意味着存在不公平地诋毁一家公司的阴谋论。

8.  *“你简直就是个雅虎/微软/谷歌/美国在线的骗子。”*-这是阴对 7 号的阳。真正有意思的是，你在看一篇负面文章对一家公司的评论时，发现一两天前，一篇正面文章对着同一家公司却留下了相反的评论。
9.  “我希望你死/我要杀了你”–这些话出现的频率超乎你的想象。有关这方面的更多信息，请参见后面的文章。在一个很棒的例子中，我们收到一位创业工程师的匿名死亡威胁。当我把它送给首席执行官时，他说:“啊，就是他，他有点奇怪。“嘿，你打算什么时候报道我们的新产品？”到目前为止，我们还没有在 TechCrunch 上再次提到这家初创公司。有些事情是不应该跨越的，威胁要杀了我们的作家，我的狗，或者我的家人就是其中之一。
10.  *“Unsubscribed!”* – A comment left after we’ve expressed an opinion counter to what the reader believes. Saying they are unsubscribing is their way of showing that they think we deserve a decline in readership. Our counter is to ban certain readers, most of whom get apoplectic and fail to realize that we have no way of stopping people from reading the site’s content.

    我们可以忍受一些读者不时因愤怒而退订，这表明我们至少让事情保持有趣。如果你真的认为我们出轨了，留下一个合理的评论，说明你为什么这么认为。我们仔细听取了这些意见。

加成:*“你删了我的评论！”*——在某人说了一些垃圾的、可恶的或愚蠢可笑的话后离开。然后读者会回来抱怨我们侵犯了他们的言论自由权，并对他们进行审查。除了他们把我们和美国政府以及他们的宪法权利混为一谈之外，他们通常是不受欢迎的，很快就会受到知识产权保护。
